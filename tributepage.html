Tribute to Shaheed Bhagat Singh

Shaheed Bhagat Singh was born on 28th of September, 1907 at Punjab. He died as a martyr at a very young age of 23. He was a revolutionary leader and contributed to the Indian freedom struggle.

Biography

Bhagat Singh was a prominent revolutionary in the Indian independence movement. Born on September 27, 1907, in Lyallpur, Punjab (now in Pakistan), he was deeply influenced by his family’s patriotic background. From a young age, Bhagat Singh was involved in revolutionary activities. He attended Dayanand Anglo Vedic High School and National College in Lahore, where he was exposed to various political ideologies. He became a vocal critic of British rule and was known for his Marxist leanings.

Achievements

  • Achievement 1: HE BECAME A LEADER OF HINDUSTAN REPUBLICAN ASSOCIATION
  • Achievement 2: HE FOUNDED NAUJAWAN BHARAT IN 1926
  • Achievement 3: HE SERVED AS AN ICON FOR REVOLUTIONARY YOUTHS ACROSS INDIA
  • Achievement 4: THE DAY OF HIS DEATH IS OBSERVED AS MARTYR’S DAY IN INDIA
